Our Philosophy

Security ratings for AI vendors are only useful if they can be trusted. That trust requires two things: independence and traceability. Every claim in a scorecard must link to something verifiable — a published policy, a certification document, or a test result from our automated suite. We do not accept vendor self-assessments, and we do not allow vendors to pay for higher scores.

We built this framework because the existing approaches fail enterprise security teams. Questionnaire-based assessments rely entirely on vendor honesty and become stale the moment they're submitted. Third-party attestations like SOC 2 cover infrastructure security but say nothing about whether a model can be manipulated into leaking sensitive data. And generic cybersecurity rating tools don't understand that an AI vendor's threat surface includes the model itself.

Our framework is designed to answer the specific question a CISO needs answered: "Is it safe to put our data and our employees into this AI system?" That question has five sub-components, each with a defined, repeatable measurement approach.

✦
Core commitment
We will never accept payment from a vendor to influence their score. Vendors may submit evidence corrections through our appeals process, but scoring decisions are made entirely by our research team based on independently verifiable evidence.

How Signals Are Collected

We gather evidence from three distinct sources. Each source has a defined scope, refresh cadence, and quality bar. Evidence that cannot be independently verified is not included in scoring.

METHOD 01
Automated crawling
Our crawler infrastructure continuously monitors public-facing vendor documentation and flags changes within 24 hours. Crawlers extract structured data from privacy policies, DPAs, trust centers, model cards, and security pages.
Privacy policies Trust centers Model cards DPA docs Cert portals GitHub repos CVE databases
METHOD 02
Active model testing
Our automated testing suite fires 90 test cases against each vendor's public API on a weekly basis. Tests cover prompt injection, jailbreak resistance, system prompt extraction, and output filter bypass. No other AI rating service does this.
Prompt injection Jailbreak attempts Extraction attacks Filter bypass Role-play escape
METHOD 03
Manual research
Our security research team reviews regulatory filings, academic vulnerability disclosures, third-party audit summaries, and news coverage. This layer catches findings that automated systems miss — especially disclosed incidents and governance changes.
Regulatory filings Academic papers Bug bounty reports Incident news SOC 2 summaries
ℹ
Evidence labeling
Every sub-signal in a vendor scorecard is labeled with its collection method — Crawled, Tested, or Researched — so you can evaluate the evidence quality yourself. Evidence links are available to Pro and Enterprise subscribers.

The 5 Dimensions

Scores are built from five weighted dimensions, totaling 100 points. Point allocations reflect the severity and frequency of risk we observe across the AI vendor landscape. Dimensions covering data handling and model behavior are weighted most heavily because they represent the largest categories of realized harm in enterprise deployments.

Grade Scale

Letter grades are assigned based on total score. The thresholds are calibrated against our full vendor dataset — the current distribution skews toward B and C, with approximately 8% of vendors holding an F grade. Grade thresholds are reviewed quarterly and versioned.

A
Approved for enterprise deployment
Vendor demonstrates strong controls across all dimensions. Minor gaps may exist but are documented and mitigated. Suitable for deployment with standard procurement review.
90–100
B
Approved with conditions
Good overall posture with identified gaps in one or two dimensions. Suitable for deployment after addressing specific findings noted in the scorecard.
75–89
C
Requires security review
Meaningful gaps present. Deployment should require formal risk acceptance, compensating controls, or escalation to your security team before proceeding.
60–74
D
High risk — escalate before deploying
Significant control failures in one or more dimensions. Deployment without explicit CISO sign-off and documented risk acceptance is strongly discouraged.
45–59
F
Do not deploy
Critical failures — lapsed certifications, blocked evidence, active security incidents, or systematic failure on model security tests. Not recommended for any enterprise use.
0–44
⚠
Grade floors
Certain conditions automatically floor a grade regardless of total score: a lapsed SOC 2 floors at C; an active undisclosed data breach floors at D; systematic jailbreak test failure (≤15/30) floors at F. These floors are documented in the individual scorecard.

Active Testing Suite

Our model security testing suite is the most differentiated part of this platform. We run 90 test cases per vendor against their public API, covering four attack categories. Tests are executed weekly from isolated infrastructure using synthetic user accounts with no access to real enterprise data.

Test design follows OWASP's LLM Top 10 and our own research into AI-specific attack patterns. We add new test cases as new attack patterns are discovered. All tests are documented, versioned, and reproducible.

ℹ
Testing infrastructure & ethics
All testing is conducted against public APIs using synthetic accounts and synthetic prompts. We do not use real user data in tests. We do not attempt to access non-public APIs or bypass authentication. Category A–C jailbreak tests use the minimum necessary specificity to determine pass/fail — we do not attempt to generate the harmful content itself.

Limitations

We believe in being direct about what this platform cannot do. Understanding these limitations will help you use scorecards as one input among several in your vendor risk process — not as a replacement for your own due diligence.

👁️
We can only see what's public Our crawlers and researchers work exclusively with publicly available information and public API endpoints. Vendors with strong internal security practices that aren't externally documented may be underscored. Conversely, vendors with good documentation but weak implementation may be overscored.
🧪
Our test suite is not exhaustive 90 test cases per vendor is a meaningful assessment but not comprehensive coverage of all possible attacks. A model that passes our suite may still be vulnerable to novel attacks. Our scores represent a defensible baseline, not a guarantee of security.
⏱️
Scores have a lag Even with continuous monitoring, there is an inherent lag between a vendor making a change and that change being reflected in their score. For critical decisions, always verify the timestamp on specific evidence items, not just the overall score update time.
🏢
Scores are organization-agnostic A single score applies to the vendor's standard product offering. Your specific deployment — custom system prompts, enterprise data isolation, specific API parameters — may be more or less secure than the baseline score reflects. Enterprise subscribers can request a custom assessment.
⚖️
This is not legal advice Scorecard findings are informational. They are not a legal determination of compliance with any regulation. Your legal and compliance teams must make their own determination about vendor suitability for specific regulatory requirements.

Appeals Process

Vendors who believe a score is based on outdated or incorrect evidence may submit an appeal. Our appeals process is designed to be transparent, timely, and resistant to commercial pressure.

What qualifies for appeal: factual errors (e.g., a certification is shown as lapsed when it was renewed), outdated information (a policy changed after our last crawl), or missing public evidence we didn't find. Appeals are not appropriate for disagreements with our scoring methodology or point allocations.

What to submit: a specific claim, the evidence URL or document, and the sub-signal you believe should change. We do not accept appeals that require us to trust unverifiable internal documentation.

Timeline: We acknowledge appeals within 2 business days and publish a decision within 10 business days. All appeal decisions and their outcomes are logged internally for consistency review.

To submit an appeal, email appeals@aisecurityscorecard.com with the subject line "Score Appeal: [Vendor Name]".

✦
Commercial independence
Appeals are reviewed by our research team independently of our sales function. Vendors who are paying customers receive no preferential treatment in the appeals process. We maintain an internal log of all appeals and outcomes that is reviewed quarterly by our advisory board.
